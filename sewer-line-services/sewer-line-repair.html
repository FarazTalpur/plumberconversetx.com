Why Quick Response Matters

Sewer line issues, if not addressed rapidly, can escalate into severe problems like:

  • Backed-Up Drains : This unsanitary condition occurs when wastewater can't flow properly, leading to unpleasant odors and potential health risks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs include slow draining sinks and bathtubs, gurgling sounds from the toilet, frequent backups and clogs, a sudden increase in your water bill, and unpleasant odors around your property.

Sewer line repair can be done through methods like traditional excavation, where trenches are dug to expose and repair the pipe, or trenchless methods such as pipe lining or pipe bursting, which are less invasive.

A repair involves fixing a specific section of the sewer line that is damaged or leaking, while a replacement involves removing and installing an entirely new line if the existing one is beyond repair.

The repair time can vary based on the extent of the damage and the method used, but most repairs can be completed within a day or two, whereas more extensive replacements might take a few days to a week.

Generally, it's not advisable to perform a sewer line repair yourself due to the complexity and potential safety risks. It's best to hire a professional plumber who has the expertise and equipment necessary for accurate diagnosis and repair.
